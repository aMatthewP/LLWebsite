Definition of Streaming

Streaming is the act of being able to consume media content and being able to play it in an instant without having to download it first. Whether it be pre-recorded or live, it can still be considered streamed content as long as it can be played on any device via the internet.

Types of Streaming Content

The common forms or types of streamed content are:

  • Video Games - is an electronic game that involves interaction with a user interface or input device to generate visual feedback from a display device.
  • Music Videos - sometimes abbreviated to MV or M/V, is a video that integrates a song or an album with imagery that is produced for promotional or musical artistic purposes.
  • TV Shows - is the general reference to any content produced for viewing on a television set that is broadcast via over-the-air, satellite, or cable.

Platforms used for Streaming

Some platforms that can be used for streaming are:

  • Twitch - Twitch is a live streaming platform for gamers. The primary focus on Twitch is video games.
  • Facebook - Facebook Live is a feature of Facebook that lets users livestream directly to the social network platform.
  • YouTube - YouTube Live assists users in broadcasting their content on a real-time basis via the YouTube channel.

Viewers Analysis for Each Streaming Category

When performing a viewers or audience analysis, we need to get their demographic information such as:

  • Gender: It is vital to supporting and progressing gender equality and will contribute substantially to lifting population health.
  • Age: To help know more about what type of content the specific age frequently watch.
  • Marital Status: Allows you to better understand certain background characteristics of an audience.
  • Nationality: To know where they are located and to know the specific content of what the country wants.
  • Education: Education is often strongly associated with fertility, mortality, and migration experiences.
  • Employment: Employment is defined as persons of working age who, during a short reference period, were engaged in any activity to produce goods or provide services for pay or profit.
  • Household Income: The combined gross income of all members of a household above a specified age.

Need of Viewers

People watch streaming content for informational and educational purposes. Games and TV shows make up streaming's entertainment aspect. On the other hand, informational/educational sources, such as the media and learning channels, convey information about things we do not currently know.

Characteristics of a Good Stream

A good stream, whether it's a live video stream, a water stream, or any other type, typically exhibits the following characteristics:

  • Consistency: A reliable and consistent flow is important. In the case of live video streams, this means a stable and uninterrupted broadcast.
  • Quality: High-resolution video and clear audio are essential for a good live video stream. In a natural water stream, water quality is important for the ecosystem it supports.
  • Engagement: Interaction with the audience or ecosystem can make a stream more engaging. In the case of live video, this might involve responding to comments or questions.
